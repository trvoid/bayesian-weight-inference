Actual Weight Inference

Prior knowledge

is what you know or belive about weight like the followings:

  1. The probability distribution of new weight follows a normal distribution centered at the prior weight.
  2. Not much changed from the prior weight.

If you belive the weight has changed much, increase the value, or else decrease it.

New evidence

is the values you obtain by measuring weight.

Comma(,) separated

Model

defines how likely measured values can be produced given an actual weight. Here we assume that the probability distribution of measured values is a normal distribution centered at the given actual weight.

If the measuring process is not stable or the scale is not accurate, increase the value, or else decrease it.

Posterior knowledge

is the updated weight by new measurements with your belief considered.
